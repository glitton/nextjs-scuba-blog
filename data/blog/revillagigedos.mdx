Scuba diving the Revillagigedos Archipelago (pronounced reh-vi-ya-heh-hih-dos, aka Socorro Islands) has been on my bucket list for some time. Thanks to the enthusiasm and great planning skills of Linda Elliott, my dream has been realized and what a trip it was!

<Image
  src="/static/images/revillagegidos_day1/g_linda.jpg"
  width={800}
  height={600}
  alt="Generosa and Linda"
/>

The trip started from Cabo San Lucas where we boarded the Nautilus Undersea vessel at 8pm on Saturday night. The boat's claim to fame is that it was a support boat for the James Bond Movie "License to Kill" and was used for the Deep Rover mission with Dr. Silvia Earle.

<Image
  src="/static/images/revillagegidos_day1/nautilus.jpg"
  width={800}
  height={600}
  alt="Nautilus Undersea boat"
/>

The plan for the week was to do our first dive in San Benedicto Island, then head on to Roca Partida then to Socorro Island and then back to do our last dives in San Benedicto Island. The captain informed us that plans can change depending on weather and dive conditions thus, flexibility and an adventurous attitude was needed.

<Image
  src="/static/images/revillagegidos_day1/chapo_revillagigedos.jpg"
  width={800}
  height={600}
  alt="Captain Oscar"
/>

We motored to San Benedicto that evening and by the end of the day Sunday, we anchored in our first dive site in San Benedicto Island.

<Image
  src="/static/images/revillagegidos_day1/san_benedicto.jpg"
  width={800}
  height={600}
  alt="slide of san benedicto island"
/>

The trip was relatively smooth with gentle rolling waves which helped me get a good night's sleep.

Our first dive was at the site called Fundadero where the goal was to check if all our gear was working, weights were appropriate, and in general to get comfortable.

<Image
  src="/static/images/revillagegidos_day1/benedicto_dive_map.jpg"
  width={800}
  height={600}
  alt="san benedicto dive map"
/>

The water temperature was around 78 degrees and viz was decent, about 40 feet. I started off wearing my chillproof Sharkskin suit which was supposed to handle temps up to 68 degrees. Alas, I was cold with just that suit thus for the next dive, I added another 2mm Lavacore layer.

The second dive was at the site called the Canyon where within the first minute under water, I saw my first manta!

  src="/static/images/revillagegidos_day1/manta-belly.jpg"

Currents were a bit strong and it kept shifting causing surges that kept me very alert and also made visibility a bit murky. I had to stragegically swim behind rocks to get protection but all in all, I was so happy to have had a great first dive day!
