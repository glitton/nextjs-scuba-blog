Cozumel has always been on my top ten list of scuba diving spots. Since I live in Puerto Morelos, Cozumel is an easy ferry ride away.

My dive buddy, Linda Elliott visited me in Puerto Morelos where we did some cenote dives which I wrote about [here](TO DO: add link to blog) and later on headed to Cozumel.

Cozumel is known for massive coral formations, variety of rays, turtles, nurse sharks, eels, and reef fish including angelfish, groupers, grunts, and more.

For this trip, I didn't have my usual Olympus OMD camera as it was acting up and at the shop. I took this opportunity to dust off my Olympus TG6 which is a very powerful camera for shooting macro. Not only did I capture nice macro but I was also able to get some decent medium and wide angle shots like the pictures below.

As advertised, the Olympus TG6 did very well with smaller macro subjects. Cozumel isn't known for macro but there are a lot of cool tiny critters. Our dive master and extraordinary photographer himself, [Juan Vega](https://www.instagram.com/juanvegaunderwater/) from [Hammerhead Cozumel](https://www.instagram.com/hammerheadcozumel/) was an awesome spotter of these little things.

He also spotted various slugs/nudibranchs

Towards the last 10 minutes of our dive, our guide spotted a [paper nautilus aka argonaut](https://oceanconservancy.org/blog/2020/10/05/paper-nautilus/) which is a type of octopus typically found on the deeper side, closer to 100 feet. He spotted the critter at around 85 feet so my buddy, Linda Elliott and me swam down to capture photos of this guy 📸 🎥 🎉.

Many of my scuba diving friends have done blackwater dives for over a year without seeing one so we were truly lucky!

Photographing this guy and other critters is not easy as they move so quickly. Fortunately I captured the nautilus on video.
