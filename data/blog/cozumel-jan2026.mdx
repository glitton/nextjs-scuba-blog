Cozumel has always been on my top ten list of scuba diving spots. Since I live in Puerto Morelos, Cozumel is an easy ferry ride away.

My dive buddy, Linda Elliott visited me in Puerto Morelos where we did some cenote dives which I will write about at some point and in the new year, we headed to Cozumel.

Cozumel is known for massive coral formations, variety of rays, turtles, nurse sharks, eels, and reef fish including angelfish, groupers, grunts, and more.

For this trip, I didn't have my usual Olympus OMD camera as it was acting up and I had to take it to the shop. I took this opportunity to dust off my Olympus TG6 which is a small but very powerful camera for shooting macro. Not only did I capture nice macro but I was also able to get some decent medium and wide angle shots like the pictures below.

As advertised, the Olympus TG6 did very well with smaller macro subjects. Cozumel isn't known for macro but there are a lot of cool tiny critters. Our dive master and an extraordinary photographer himself, [Juan Vega](https://www.instagram.com/juanvegaunderwater/) from [Hammerhead Cozumel](https://www.instagram.com/hammerheadcozumel/) was an awesome spotter of these little things.
